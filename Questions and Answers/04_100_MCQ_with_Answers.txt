## SECTION A: FUNDAMENTALS & PROPERTIES (Questions 1-20)

**Q1:** Which of the following is a scalar quantity in fluid mechanics?
A) Velocity
B) Pressure
C) Acceleration
D) Momentum
**Answer: B) Pressure**
*Explanation: Pressure is a scalar (force per unit area); same in all directions at a point*

**Q2:** An ideal fluid is characterized by:
A) High viscosity
B) Frictionless and incompressible
C) High density
D) Low Reynolds number
**Answer: B) Frictionless and incompressible**

**Q3:** The SI unit of dynamic viscosity is:
A) Poise
B) Stoke
C) Pascal-second
D) Newton per meter
**Answer: C) Pascal-second**
*Explanation: 1 Pa·s = 10 poise*

**Q4:** Kinematic viscosity is defined as:
A) Viscosity × density
B) Viscosity / density
C) Density / viscosity
D) Viscosity × area
**Answer: B) Viscosity / density**
*Formula: ν = μ/ρ*

**Q5:** The viscosity of gases _____ with increase in temperature.
A) Decreases
B) Increases
C) Remains constant
D) Becomes zero
**Answer: B) Increases**
*Liquids show opposite behavior (viscosity decreases with temperature)*

**Q6:** Surface tension acts at the _____ due to _____.
A) Liquid surface; gravity
B) Liquid-gas interface; unbalanced molecular forces
C) Bottom of container; pressure
D) Center of mass; inertia
**Answer: B) Liquid-gas interface; unbalanced molecular forces**

**Q7:** Which of the following represents bulk modulus correctly?
A) Change in pressure / Fractional change in volume
B) Change in volume / Change in pressure
C) Density × Velocity
D) Pressure × Volume
**Answer: A) Change in pressure / Fractional change in volume**
*Formula: K = -ΔP / (ΔV/V)*

**Q8:** Water has higher bulk modulus than air because:
A) Water is heavier
B) Water molecules are more tightly bound
C) Water has higher velocity of sound
D) Water has lower viscosity
**Answer: B) Water molecules are more tightly bound**

**Q9:** If dynamic viscosity of a fluid is 0.8 poise and specific gravity is 0.8, kinematic viscosity in stokes is:
A) 0.8
B) 1.0
C) 0.64
D) 6.4
**Answer: B) 1.0**
*Calculation: ν = μ/ρ = 0.8 poise / 0.8 = 1.0 stoke*

**Q10:** Vapor pressure becomes important in fluid mechanics due to:
A) Boiling of fluid
B) Cavitation in pumps
C) Surface tension
D) Density variation
**Answer: B) Cavitation in pumps**
*When local pressure < vapor pressure, bubbles form (cavitation)*

**Q11:** The property that determines whether a flow is compressible or incompressible is:
A) Pressure gradient
B) Mach number
C) Reynolds number
D) Froude number
**Answer: B) Mach number**
*If M < 0.3, flow can be treated as incompressible*

**Q12:** A Newtonian fluid is one that:
A) Obeys Newton's law of motion
B) Follows linear relationship between shear stress and rate of strain
C) Has zero viscosity
D) Has constant density
**Answer: B) Follows linear relationship between shear stress and rate of strain**
*τ = μ(du/dy)*

**Q13:** Among the following, which is a non-Newtonian fluid?
A) Water
B) Air
C) Oil
D) Ketchup
**Answer: D) Ketchup**
*Ketchup is pseudoplastic (shear-thinning)*

**Q14:** The modulus of elasticity for water is approximately:
A) 0.1 × 10⁵ Pa
B) 1 × 10⁵ Pa
C) 2.2 × 10⁹ Pa
D) 1 × 10¹² Pa
**Answer: C) 2.2 × 10⁹ Pa**
*Water is nearly incompressible*

**Q15:** Which property of fluid increases with increase in altitude?
A) Density
B) Pressure
C) Temperature
D) Viscosity
**Answer: None directly (density and pressure both decrease)*
*This question tests understanding that density and pressure decrease with altitude*

**Q16:** Pascal is the unit of:
A) Viscosity
B) Pressure
C) Kinematic viscosity
D) Surface tension
**Answer: B) Pressure**
*1 Pa = 1 N/m²*

**Q17:** Stoke is the unit of:
A) Dynamic viscosity
B) Kinematic viscosity
C) Surface tension
D) Vapor pressure
**Answer: B) Kinematic viscosity**
*1 stoke = 1 cm²/s = 10⁻⁴ m²/s*

**Q18:** The ratio of specific heats for an ideal gas is denoted by:
A) μ
B) ν
C) γ (gamma)
D) σ
**Answer: C) γ (gamma)**
*γ = c_p/c_v; for air, γ ≈ 1.4*

**Q19:** Compressibility factor is highest for:
A) Water
B) Oil
C) Air
D) Mercury
**Answer: C) Air**
*Air is most compressible among the options*

**Q20:** Which statement about surface tension is correct?
A) It increases with temperature
B) It decreases with temperature
C) It is independent of temperature
D) It varies randomly
**Answer: B) It decreases with temperature**

## SECTION B: FLUID STATICS & PRESSURE (Questions 21-35)

**Q21:** Pascal's law states that:
A) Pressure in a fluid varies with height
B) Pressure applied to enclosed fluid is transmitted undiminished
C) Pressure is zero at free surface
D) Pressure increases linearly with depth
**Answer: B) Pressure applied to enclosed fluid is transmitted undiminished**

**Q22:** The pressure at a depth h below the free surface is:
A) ρgh
B) P_atm + ρgh
C) P_atm - ρgh
D) ρh
**Answer: B) P_atm + ρgh**

**Q23:** For a U-tube manometer with liquid of density ρ, if height difference is h, pressure difference is:
A) ρh
B) ρgh
C) h/ρ
D) gh
**Answer: B) ρgh**

**Q24:** A barometer measures:
A) Gauge pressure
B) Vacuum pressure
C) Atmospheric pressure
D) Vapor pressure
**Answer: C) Atmospheric pressure**

**Q25:** Hydrostatic pressure acts:
A) Only vertically downward
B) Only horizontally
C) Equally in all directions at a point
D) Only perpendicular to surface
**Answer: C) Equally in all directions at a point**

**Q26:** The center of pressure is:
A) At the centroid of the submerged surface
B) Always below the centroid
C) Always above the centroid
D) At the geometric center
**Answer: B) Always below the centroid**
*Due to pressure variation with depth*

**Q27:** Buoyant force acts through:
A) Center of mass
B) Centroid of submerged volume
C) Center of pressure
D) Free surface
**Answer: B) Centroid of submerged volume**

**Q28:** For a floating body in equilibrium:
A) Weight = Buoyant force
B) Weight > Buoyant force
C) Weight < Buoyant force
D) There is no relation
**Answer: A) Weight = Buoyant force**

**Q29:** The metacenter is important for:
A) Pipe design
B) Pump selection
C) Floating body stability
D) Pressure measurement
**Answer: C) Floating body stability**

**Q30:** Absolute pressure is:
A) Gauge pressure + Atmospheric pressure
B) Gauge pressure - Atmospheric pressure
C) Atmospheric pressure only
D) Vapor pressure
**Answer: A) Gauge pressure + Atmospheric pressure**

**Q31:** A vacuum pressure of -40 kPa (gauge) with atmospheric pressure 100 kPa means absolute pressure is:
A) 40 kPa
B) 60 kPa
C) 140 kPa
D) 100 kPa
**Answer: B) 60 kPa**
*P_absolute = 100 - 40 = 60 kPa*

**Q32:** The pressure coefficient C_p = (P - P_∞)/(0.5ρV_∞²) has dimensions:
A) Pressure
B) Dimensionless
C) Velocity
D) Acceleration
**Answer: B) Dimensionless**

**Q33:** At stagnation point on a body moving through fluid, C_p = :
A) 0
B) 1
C) -1
D) 0.5
**Answer: B) 1**
*At stagnation, all kinetic energy converts to pressure*

**Q34:** The pressure head is expressed as:
A) P/ρ
B) P/(ρg)
C) ρgP
D) P/(ρ²)
**Answer: B) P/(ρg)**
*Pressure head h = P/(ρg) in meters*

**Q35:** In a manometer, the liquid with highest density would give:
A) Highest sensitivity
B) Lowest sensitivity
C) No difference
D) Immeasurable reading
**Answer: B) Lowest sensitivity**
*Smaller height difference for same pressure difference*

## SECTION C: CONTINUITY & BERNOULLI (Questions 36-50)

**Q36:** The continuity equation for steady incompressible flow is:
A) A₁V₁ = A₂V₂
B) ρA₁V₁ = ρA₂V₂
C) P₁/ρ + V₁²/2 = P₂/ρ + V₂²/2
D) F = ρAV
**Answer: A) A₁V₁ = A₂V₂**

**Q37:** For flow through a convergent nozzle, velocity:
A) Decreases
B) Remains constant
C) Increases
D) Becomes zero
**Answer: C) Increases**
*Area decreases, so velocity increases (continuity)*

**Q38:** Bernoulli's equation is valid for:
A) All flows
B) Ideal, incompressible, steady flow along a streamline
C) Turbulent flow only
D) Viscous flow
**Answer: B) Ideal, incompressible, steady flow along a streamline**

**Q39:** In Bernoulli's equation P/ρg + V²/2g + z = constant, the term z represents:
A) Pressure head
B) Velocity head
C) Elevation head
D) Dynamic head
**Answer: C) Elevation head**

**Q40:** If pressure decreases along a streamline (ideal flow), velocity must:
A) Decrease
B) Increase
C) Remain constant
D) Become zero
**Answer: B) Increase**
*Lower pressure → lower pressure head → higher velocity*

**Q41:** A horizontal pipe carries water. If diameter reduces from 0.1 m to 0.05 m, velocity increases by factor:
A) 2
B) 4
C) √2
D) 0.5
**Answer: B) 4**
*A₁V₁ = A₂V₂; (D₁/D₂)² = (0.1/0.05)² = 4*

**Q42:** Water flows from large tank through orifice 5 m below surface. Exit velocity is approximately:
A) √(2gh) = √(2×9.81×5) ≈ 9.9 m/s
B) √(gh)
C) gh
D) 2√(gh)
**Answer: A) √(2gh) ≈ 9.9 m/s**
*Torricelli's law; h = V²/2g*

**Q43:** For siphon starting 1 m above water and ending 2 m below surface, maximum height for siphon to work is:
A) 1 m
B) 10.33 m
C) 5 m
D) 2 m
**Answer: B) 10.33 m**
*Maximum suction height = atmospheric pressure head ≈ 10.33 m*

**Q44:** In a Venturi meter, the narrowest section:
A) Has highest pressure
B) Has lowest pressure
C) Has zero velocity
D) Has maximum pressure
**Answer: B) Has lowest pressure**
*Velocity highest there, so pressure lowest (Bernoulli)*

**Q45:** The principle behind the Venturi meter is:
A) Pascal's law
B) Archimedes' principle
C) Bernoulli's equation
D) Continuity equation
**Answer: C) Bernoulli's equation**

**Q46:** Energy loss due to friction in Bernoulli equation is represented by:
A) h_L (head loss)
B) P/ρg
C) V²/2g
D) z
**Answer: A) h_L (head loss)**

**Q47:** In turbulent pipe flow, velocity profile is:
A) Linear
B) Parabolic
C) Logarithmic
D) Exponential
**Answer: C) Logarithmic**
*Near-wall region shows log profile; laminar is parabolic*

**Q48:** The discharge coefficient for an orifice is:
A) Always 1.0
B) Less than 1.0 (typically 0.6-0.7)
C) Greater than 1.0
D) Zero
**Answer: B) Less than 1.0**
*Accounts for vena contracta and friction*

**Q49:** For ideal flow with no losses, mechanical energy is:
A) Gained
B) Lost
C) Conserved
D) Converted to heat
**Answer: C) Conserved**

**Q50:** In potential flow around a cylinder, at the sides (θ = 90°), the pressure coefficient is:
A) 1
B) 0
C) -1
D) -3
**Answer: D) -3**

## SECTION D: FLOW CLASSIFICATION & REYNOLDS NUMBER (Questions 51-65)

**Q51:** Reynolds number is defined as:
A) V/√(gD)
B) ρVD/μ
C) V/a (speed of sound)
D) Force/Area
**Answer: B) ρVD/μ**

**Q52:** For flow in a circular pipe, laminar flow occurs when Re is:
A) < 2,300
B) > 4,000
C) > 2,300
D) Any value
**Answer: A) < 2,300**

**Q53:** Water (ν = 10⁻⁶ m²/s) flows through 0.05 m pipe at 2 m/s. Reynolds number is:
A) 100,000
B) 50,000
C) 10,000
D) 1,000
**Answer: A) 100,000**
*Re = VD/ν = (2×0.05)/10⁻⁶ = 100,000*

**Q54:** In laminar flow through a pipe, friction factor is given by:
A) f = 64/Re
B) f = 0.316/Re^0.25
C) f = 0.025
D) f is constant
**Answer: A) f = 64/Re**

**Q55:** Moody diagram is used to find:
A) Velocity profile
B) Pressure drop
C) Friction factor
D) Reynolds number
**Answer: C) Friction factor**

**Q56:** Transition to turbulent flow begins when Re > :
A) 1,000
B) 2,300
C) 4,000
D) 10,000
**Answer: B) 2,300**

**Q57:** For a steady flow, properties at a given location:
A) Change with time
B) Do not change with time
C) Oscillate
D) Become zero
**Answer: B) Do not change with time**

**Q58:** Uniform flow means:
A) Flow is steady
B) Velocity is same everywhere in field
C) No turbulence
D) Constant viscosity
**Answer: B) Velocity is same everywhere in field**

**Q59:** One-dimensional flow is an assumption where properties depend on:
A) All three coordinates (x, y, z)
B) Two coordinates (x, y)
C) One coordinate only
D) Time only
**Answer: C) One coordinate only**

**Q60:** Stokes flow (creeping flow) occurs at:
A) High Reynolds number
B) Low Reynolds number (Re << 1)
C) Re ≈ 1
D) Transitional flow
**Answer: B) Low Reynolds number (Re << 1)**

**Q61:** In irrotational flow:
A) Vorticity = 0
B) Velocity = 0
C) Circulation ≠ 0
D) Particles rotate about own axis
**Answer: A) Vorticity = 0**

**Q62:** For entrance flow in a pipe, entrance length for laminar flow is:
A) 0.05 × Re × D
B) 4.4 × Re^(1/6) × D
C) 0.1 × D
D) L/D = constant
**Answer: A) 0.05 × Re × D**

**Q63:** In fully developed flow through a pipe:
A) Velocity profile changes along length
B) Velocity profile is constant along length
C) Velocity is uniform across section
D) No velocity gradient
**Answer: B) Velocity profile is constant along length**

**Q64:** For laminar flow between parallel plates, velocity profile is:
A) Linear
B) Parabolic
C) Uniform
D) Logarithmic
**Answer: B) Parabolic**

**Q65:** The Colebrook-White equation is used to find:
A) Reynolds number
B) Friction factor in turbulent flow
C) Pressure drop
D) Viscosity
**Answer: B) Friction factor in turbulent flow**

## SECTION E: MOMENTUM & FORCES (Questions 66-80)

**Q66:** The momentum equation states:
A) F = ma (Newton's 2nd law)
B) Force = Rate of change of momentum
C) Momentum is conserved
D) All of above
**Answer: D) All of above**

**Q67:** For steady flow control volume: ΣF = :
A) ṁ × V
B) ṁ(V₂ - V₁)
C) ρ × V
D) ρ/A
**Answer: B) ṁ(V₂ - V₁)**

**Q68:** A water jet (V = 10 m/s, A = 0.01 m²) hits a plate perpendicularly. Force on plate is:
A) 100 N
B) 1000 N
C) 10,000 N
D) 1 N
**Answer: B) 1000 N**
*F = ρAV² = 1000×0.01×10² = 1000 N*

**Q69:** For a bend in pipe carrying flow, the resultant force on the bend is due to:
A) Pressure forces
B) Momentum change
C) Weight
D) Both A and B
**Answer: D) Both A and B**

**Q70:** In a 90° elbow, if flow enters at velocity V, momentum change is:
A) V
B) √2 × V
C) 2V
D) 0
**Answer: B) √2 × V**
*Vector addition: √(V² + V²) = √2V*

**Q71:** The drag force on a body is related to velocity as:
A) F_D ∝ V
B) F_D ∝ V²
C) F_D ∝ V³
D) F_D ∝ 1/V
**Answer: B) F_D ∝ V²**

**Q72:** Drag coefficient C_D = F_D / (ρV²A/2) is:
A) Dimensional
B) Dimensionless
C) Has units of pressure
D) Has units of velocity
**Answer: B) Dimensionless**

**Q73:** For a sphere, drag coefficient at high Re is approximately:
A) 0.1
B) 0.47
C) 1.2
D) 2.0
**Answer: B) 0.47**

**Q74:** The lift force on an airfoil is primarily due to:
A) Drag
B) Pressure difference between upper and lower surfaces
C) Weight
D) Inertial forces only
**Answer: B) Pressure difference between upper and lower surfaces**

**Q75:** Magnus effect (curve ball) is caused by:
A) Weight
B) Pressure difference due to rotatio**Answer: B) Pressure difference due to rotation and relative motion**

## SECTION F: PIPE FLOW & APPLICATIONS (Questions 81-100)

**Q81:** Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure drop is:
A) ΔP = f(L/D)(ρV²/2)
B) ΔP = ρgh
C) ΔP = V²/2
D) ΔP = μV/D
**Answer: A) ΔP = f(L/D)(ρV²/2)**

**Q82:** Head loss in a pipe increases with:
A) Decrease in length
B) Increase in velocity
C) Increase in diameter
D) Decrease in friction factor
**Answer: B) Increase in velocity**
*h_f ∝ V² (directly proportional)*

**Q83:** In laminar pipe flow, friction factor f is independent of:
A) Viscosity
B) Density
C) Pipe roughness
D) Reynolds number
**Answer: C) Pipe roughness**
*For laminar: f = 64/Re (independent of roughness)*

**Q84:** Minor losses in pipes include losses due to:
A) Friction in straight sections
B) Bends, valves, fittings, entrance, exit
C) Fluid weight
D) Density changes
**Answer: B) Bends, valves, fittings, entrance, exit**

**Q85:** Loss coefficient for sudden expansion from A₁ to A₂ is:
A) (A₁/A₂)²
B) (1 - A₁/A₂)²
C) (A₂/A₁)²
D) √(A₂/A₁)
**Answer: B) (1 - A₁/A₂)²**

**Q86:** For a fully open valve, loss coefficient is approximately:
A) 0.0
B) 0.2
C) 0.5
D) 1.0
**Answer: A) 0.0**
*No loss for fully open valve*

**Q87:** For a 90° elbow in pipe, loss coefficient K is approximately:
A) 0.2
B) 0.5
C) 0.9
D) 1.5
**Answer: C) 0.9**

**Q88:** Manning's equation for open channel is used with:
A) Manning's n (roughness coefficient)
B) Hydraulic radius R
C) Channel slope S
D) All of above
**Answer: D) All of above**
*V = (1/n)R^(2/3)S^(1/2)*

**Q89:** Critical depth in open channel occurs when:
A) Froude number = 0
B) Froude number = 1
C) Froude number > 1
D) Reynolds number = 1
**Answer: B) Froude number = 1**

**Q90:** A hydraulic jump occurs when flow transitions from:
A) Laminar to turbulent
B) Subcritical to supercritical
C) Supercritical to subcritical
D) Steady to unsteady
**Answer: C) Supercritical to subcritical**

**Q91:** Pressure drop in turbulent pipe flow is approximately:
A) Proportional to V
B) Proportional to V²
C) Proportional to V^(1.75)
D) Independent of V
**Answer: B) Proportional to V²**

**Q92:** Pump power required is given by:
A) W = ρgQH
B) W = ρgQ(H + losses)
C) W = ρgQ/H
D) W = ρQ
**Answer: A) W = ρgQH**
*(for ideal pump without losses)*

**Q93:** For turbine, specific speed N_s relates:
A) Speed and power
B) Speed and head
C) Speed, power and head
D) Only head
**Answer: C) Speed, power and head**
*N_s = N√P / H^(5/4)*

**Q94:** Cavitation in pumps occurs when:
A) Pressure increases
B) Local pressure falls below vapor pressure
C) Velocity increases
D) Temperature increases
**Answer: B) Local pressure falls below vapor pressure**

**Q95:** NPSH (Net Positive Suction Head) is important for:
A) Preventing cavitation
B) Increasing flow rate
C) Reducing power
D) Lowering discharge
**Answer: A) Preventing cavitation**

**Q96:** Pipe diameter for given flow and pressure drop is determined using:
A) Bernoulli's equation alone
B) Darcy-Weisbach equation
C) Continuity and Darcy-Weisbach
D) Energy equation alone
**Answer: C) Continuity and Darcy-Weisbach**

**Q97:** In series pipes, pressure drop:
A) Is same in all pipes
B) Depends on friction factor and length
C) Is proportional to length/diameter ratio
D) Is proportional to velocity squared
**Answer: B) Depends on friction factor and length**

**Q98:** For parallel pipes, flow distribution:
A) Is equal in each pipe
B) Depends on relative resistances
C) Is proportional to diameter
D) Is proportional to area
**Answer: B) Depends on relative resistances**

**Q99:** The velocity profile for turbulent pipe flow near the wall is:
A) Linear
B) Parabolic
C) Logarithmic
D) Exponential
**Answer: C) Logarithmic**

**Q100:** Equivalent length of a fitting is:
A) Physical length
B) Length of pipe producing same loss
C) Always longer than fitting
D) Always shorter than fitting
**Answer: B) Length of pipe producing same loss**
*Used as L_eq = (K/f) × D*
