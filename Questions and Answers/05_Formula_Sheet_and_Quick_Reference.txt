## 1. FUNDAMENTAL DEFINITIONS

### Density & Specific Gravity
- **Density:** ρ = m/V (kg/m³)
- **Specific Gravity:** s = ρ_fluid / ρ_water = (dimensionless)
- **Water at 4°C:** ρ = 1000 kg/m³ (reference)

### Viscosity
- **Dynamic Viscosity:** τ = μ(du/dy) (Pa·s or N·s/m²)
- **Kinematic Viscosity:** ν = μ/ρ (m²/s)
- **Unit Conversions:** 1 Pa·s = 10 poise; 1 m²/s = 10⁴ stoke

### Bulk Modulus & Compressibility
- **Bulk Modulus:** K = -ΔP/(ΔV/V) = ρ(dP/dρ) (Pa)
- **Speed of Sound:** a = √(K/ρ) = √(γRT) for gases
- **Water:** K ≈ 2.2 × 10⁹ Pa; **Air:** K ≈ 1.01 × 10⁵ Pa

### Surface Tension
- **Surface Tension:** σ (N/m)
- **Capillary Rise:** h = (4σ cos θ)/(ρgD)
- **Pressure Jump (Bubble):** ΔP = 4σ/D

---

## 2. FLUID STATICS

### Pressure Variation with Depth
- **Hydrostatic Pressure:** P = P_atm + ρgh
- **Pressure Head:** h = P/(ρg) (meters of fluid)
- **Gauge Pressure:** P_gauge = P_absolute - P_atm

### Manometer Equations
- **Simple U-tube:** P₁ + ρgh₁ = P₂ + ρgh₂
- **Pressure Difference:** ΔP = ρg(h₁ - h₂)
- **For Multiple Liquids:** Account for each liquid separately

### Hydrostatic Force on Surfaces
- **Total Force:** F = P_c × A = ρg(h_c) × A
  - h_c = depth of centroid
  - A = area of surface
  
- **Center of Pressure:** h_p = h_c + I_g/(A × h_c)
  - I_g = second moment about centroid
  - Always below centroid (h_p > h_c)

### Archimedes' Principle
- **Buoyant Force:** F_B = ρ_fluid × V_displaced × g
- **For Floating Bodies:** Weight = Buoyant Force
- **Metacentric Height:** GM = I/V (stability measure)

---

## 3. CONTINUITY EQUATION

### Incompressible Flow
- **Continuity:** Q = A₁V₁ = A₂V₂ = constant
- **Volumetric Flow Rate:** Q = A × V (m³/s)
- **Mass Flow Rate:** ṁ = ρ × Q (kg/s)

### Compressible Flow
- **Mass Conservation:** ρ₁A₁V₁ = ρ₂A₂V₂ = ṁ (constant)
- **Mach Number:** M = V/a (determines compressibility effects)

### Control Volume Continuity
- **Steady Flow:** Σ(ṁ_in) = Σ(ṁ_out)
- **∫∫(ρV·dA) = 0** over entire control surface

---

## 4. BERNOULLI'S EQUATION

### Ideal Flow (No Losses)
- **Along Streamline:** P₁/ρg + V₁²/2g + z₁ = P₂/ρg + V₂²/2g + z₂
- **In Meters of Fluid:** P/ρg (pressure head), V²/2g (velocity head), z (elevation head)
- **Alternative Form:** P/ρ + V²/2 + gz = constant (energy per unit mass)

### Real Flow (With Losses)
- **Modified:** P₁/ρg + V₁²/2g + z₁ = P₂/ρg + V₂²/2g + z₂ + h_L
- **Head Loss:** h_L = friction loss + minor losses (m)

### Special Cases
- **Horizontal Pipe:** P₁/ρ + V₁²/2 = P₂/ρ + V₂²/2
- **At Rest (V=0):** P₁/ρg + z₁ = P₂/ρg + z₂ (hydrostatic)
- **Tank Discharge:** V = √(2gh) (Torricelli's law)

---

## 5. MOMENTUM EQUATION

### Control Volume Momentum
- **Steady Flow:** ΣF_ext = Σ(ṁV)_out - Σ(ṁV)_in
- **Component Form:** F_x = ṁ(V₂ₓ - V₁ₓ)
- **Force on Pipe:** Include pressure forces on boundaries

### Common Applications
- **Jet Force on Plate:** F = ρAV² (perpendicular impact)
- **Force on Bend:** F = ṁ|V₂ - V₁| (vector difference)
- **Nozzle Thrust:** F = ρA₁V₁(V₂ - V₁)

### Reynolds Transport Theorem
- **d/dt∫∫∫CV(ρβ)dV + ∫∫CS(ρβV·n)dA = ΣQ**

---

## 6. ENERGY EQUATION

### First Law of Thermodynamics (Flow)
- **Per Unit Mass:** P₁/ρ + V₁²/2 + gz₁ + q - w = P₂/ρ + V₂²/2 + gz₂ + losses
- **Pump:** W_pump = ρgQ(H) (Watts, without losses)
- **Turbine:** W_turbine = ρgQ(H) (Power extracted)

### Shaft Power
- **Pump Power:** W_p = ṁg(H_p) = ρgQH_p
- **Turbine Power:** W_t = ṁg(H_t) = ρgQH_t
- **Efficiency:** η = W_actual / W_theoretical

## 7. DIMENSIONAL ANALYSIS & SIMILARITY

### Dimensionless Numbers

| Number | Definition | Significance |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Reynolds (Re)** | ρVD/μ = VD/ν | Inertial/Viscous; flow regime |
| **Froude (Fr)** | V/√(gD) | Inertial/Gravity; wave effects |
| **Mach (M)** | V/a | Inertial/Elastic; compressibility |
| **Drag Coeff (C_D)** | F_D/(ρV²A/2) | Aerodynamic drag |
| **Lift Coeff (C_L)** | F_L/(ρV²A/2) | Aerodynamic lift |
| **Pressure Coeff (C_p)** | (P-P_∞)/(ρV²/2) | Pressure distribution |
| **Strouhal (St)** | fD/V | Oscillation frequency |

### Scaling Laws
- **Dynamic Similarity:** Same Fr, Re, M for model and prototype
- **Froude Scaling (gravity effects):** V_m/V_p = √(L_m/L_p)
- **Reynolds Scaling (viscous effects):** ν_m × L_m/V_m = ν_p × L_p/V_p

## 8. PIPE FLOW - FRICTION

### Friction Factor Correlations

**For Laminar Flow (Re < 2,300):**
- **f = 64/Re** (exact)
- Independent of pipe roughness

**For Turbulent Flow (Re > 4,000):**
- **Blasius (smooth pipe, Re < 100,000):** f = 0.316/Re^0.25
- **Colebrook-White (implicit):** 1/√f = -2 log(ε/3.7D + 2.51/Re√f)
- **Haaland (explicit):** 1/√f = -1.8 log[(ε/3.7D)^1.11 + 6.9/Re]

**Where:**
- ε = absolute roughness (m)
- D = pipe diameter (m)

### Darcy-Weisbach Equation
- **Pressure Drop:** ΔP = f(L/D)(ρV²/2)
- **Head Loss:** h_f = f(L/D)(V²/2g)
- **Alternatively:** Q = -√[2gΔh/f(L/D)]

### Minor Losses
- **Formula:** h_minor = K(V²/2g) or ΔP_minor = K(ρV²/2)
- **Loss Coefficients (K):**
  - Entrance (sharp): K ≈ 0.5
  - Entrance (rounded): K ≈ 0.1
  - Exit: K = 1.0
  - 90° elbow: K ≈ 0.9 (long radius: 0.6)
  - 45° elbow: K ≈ 0.4
  - Sudden expansion: K = (1 - A₁/A₂)²
  - Sudden contraction: K ≈ 0.4
  - Fully open valve: K ≈ 0 (or 0.2-0.3 for globe)

### Total Head Loss
- **h_total = h_friction + Σh_minor = f(L/D)(V²/2g) + ΣK(V²/2g)**

## 9. REYNOLDS NUMBER & FLOW CLASSIFICATION

### Reynolds Number
- **Re = ρVD/μ = VD/ν**
- **D** = characteristic length (pipe diameter, length of object)

### Flow Regimes in Pipes
- **Laminar:** Re < 2,300
- **Transitional:** 2,300 < Re < 4,000
- **Turbulent:** Re > 4,000

### Velocity Profiles
- **Laminar:** u(r) = 2V_avg(1 - r²/R²) (parabolic)
- **Turbulent:** u(r) ≈ u_max(1 - r/R)^(1/n), n ≈ 6-10

---

## 10. OPEN CHANNEL FLOW

### Manning's Equation
- **V = (1/n)R_h^(2/3)S^(1/2)**
- **Q = (A/n)R_h^(2/3)S^(1/2)**
- Where:
  - n = Manning's roughness coefficient (0.01-0.05)
  - R_h = A/P = hydraulic radius
  - S = slope (rise/run)

### Critical Flow
- **Froude Number:** Fr = V/√(gy) = 1 (at critical depth)
- **Critical Depth (rectangular):** y_c = ∛(q²/g)
- **Critical Slope:** S_c = (n²g)/(R_h^(1/3))

### Flow Classification
- **Subcritical:** Fr < 1, y > y_c (slow, deep)
- **Critical:** Fr = 1, y = y_c
- **Supercritical:** Fr > 1, y < y_c (fast, shallow)

### Hydraulic Jump
- **Depth Relation:** y₂/y₁ = 0.5[√(1 + 8Fr₁²) - 1]
- **Energy Loss:** h_L = (y₂ - y₁)³/(4y₁y₂)

---

## 11. PUMPS & TURBINES

### Pump Head
- **Total Head:** H_pump = (P₂ - P₁)/ρg + (V₂² - V₁²)/2g + (z₂ - z₁)
- **Pump Power:** W = ρgQH (ideal, no losses)
- **Brake Horsepower:** BHP = W/η (with efficiency)

### Specific Speed
- **N_s = N√Q/H^(3/4)** (metric units)
- Determines pump type:
  - N_s < 10: Reciprocating
  - 10 < N_s < 50: Centrifugal
  - 50 < N_s < 150: Mixed flow
  - N_s > 150: Axial flow

### Net Positive Suction Head (NPSH)
- **Required:** NPSH_req (from pump manufacturer)
- **Available:** NPSH_a = (P_atm - P_vapor)/ρg - h_f - V_inlet²/2g
- **Criterion:** NPSH_a > NPSH_req (to prevent cavitation)

---

## 12. DRAG & LIFT

### Drag Force
- **F_D = C_D × (ρV²/2) × A**
- **Components:**
  - Pressure drag (form drag)
  - Skin friction drag
  - Wave drag (for free surfaces)

### Lift Force
- **F_L = C_L × (ρV²/2) × A**
- For airfoils and curved surfaces

### Typical Drag Coefficients
- **Smooth sphere:** C_D ≈ 0.47 (Re > 1000)
- **Cylinder (circular):** C_D ≈ 1.2 (Re = 10⁴)
- **Flat plate (normal):** C_D ≈ 1.3
- **Car:** C_D ≈ 0.25-0.35
- **Streamlined body:** C_D ≈ 0.05-0.1

---

## 13. COMPRESSIBLE FLOW

### Mach Number
- **M = V/a** (dimensionless)
- **a = √(γRT)** for ideal gas
- **Flow:** Incompressible if M < 0.3

### Speed of Sound
- **a = √(K/ρ) = √(γRT)**
- **Water:** a ≈ 1480 m/s
- **Air (20°C):** a ≈ 343 m/s

### Isentropic Relations
- **T₂/T₁ = (P₂/P₁)^((γ-1)/γ)**
- **ρ₂/ρ₁ = (P₂/P₁)^(1/γ)**

---

## 14. BOUNDARY LAYER

### Boundary Layer Thickness
- **Laminar:** δ/x ≈ 5/√Re_x
- **Turbulent:** δ/x ≈ 0.37/Re_x^(1/5)

### Shear Stress
- **τ_wall = (1/2)ρV²C_f**
- **C_f = (drag coeff) for flat plate**

## COMMON PROBLEM-SOLVING SEQUENCE

1. **Draw diagram** with control volumes, sections, and unknowns
2. **List assumptions:** steady, incompressible, ideal/real, etc.
3. **Apply continuity:** A₁V₁ = A₂V₂
4. **Apply Bernoulli or energy:** P₁/ρg + V₁²/2g + z₁ = P₂/ρg + V₂²/2g + z₂ + h_L
5. **Apply momentum:** ΣF = ṁ(V₂ - V₁)
6. **Calculate friction:** ΔP = f(L/D)(ρV²/2)
7. **Check dimensions** and reasonableness
8. **Verify units** in final answer
