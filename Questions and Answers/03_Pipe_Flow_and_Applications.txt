## 2. HEAD LOSS IN PIPES

### Q5: What are the types of head loss in pipe systems? Explain major and minor losses.
**A:**

**Total Head Loss = Major Loss + Minor Loss**

**Major Loss (Friction Loss):**
- Due to friction between fluid and pipe walls in straight sections
- Formula: h_f = f(L/D)(Vآ²/2g)
- Increases with:
  - Pipe length (L)
  - Friction factor (f)
  - Velocity squared (Vآ²)
- Decreases with:
  - Pipe diameter (D)

**Minor Losses:**
- Due to flow disturbances (bends, valves, fittings, entrance, exit)
- Formula: h_m = K(Vآ²/2g)
  - K = loss coefficient (depends on type of fitting)
- Examples:
  - Sudden expansion: K â‰ˆ (1 - Aâ‚پ/Aâ‚‚)آ²

  - 90آ° elbow: K â‰ˆ 0.9
  - Fully open valve: K â‰ˆ 0
  - Pipe entrance: K â‰ˆ 0.5
  - Pipe exit: K = 1.0

## 5. FLOW AROUND OBJECTS

### Q11: What are drag and lift forces? Explain the components of drag.
**A:**

**Drag Force (F_D):**
- Resistance force in direction of flow
- Coefficient: C_D = F_D / (دپVآ²A/2)
- Typical values: C_D = 0.1-2.0 depending on shape

**Lift Force (F_L):**
- Force perpendicular to flow direction
- Coefficient: C_L = F_L / (دپVآ²A/2)
- Occurs on curved surfaces (airfoils) due to pressure difference

**Components of Drag:**

1. **Pressure Drag (Form Drag):**
   - Due to pressure difference between front and rear
   - Dominant at high Re (turbulent)
   - Increases with bluff shape
   
2. **Skin Friction Drag (Viscous Drag):**
   - Due to shear stress at surface
   - Dominant at low Re (laminar)
   - Increases with surface area
   
3. **Wave Drag (at surfaces):**
   - Due to wave formation (liquid surfaces, compressible flow)
   - Relevant for ships and high-speed vehicles

**Total Drag:**
- C_D(total) = C_D(pressure) + C_D(friction)

**Examples:**
- Smooth sphere: C_D â‰ˆ 0.47 (Re > 1000)
- Cylinder: C_D â‰ˆ 1.2
- Car: C_D â‰ˆ 0.3
- Flat plate: C_D â‰ˆ 1.3

## 6. OPEN CHANNEL FLOW

### Q12: Define critical depth and critical slope in open channel flow.
**A:**

**Critical Depth (y_c):**
- Depth at which specific energy is minimum for given flow rate
- Flow transitions from subcritical to supercritical
- Froude number: Fr = 1 at critical depth
- Formula (rectangular channel):
  y_c = âˆ›(qآ²/g)
  - q = Q/b = discharge per unit width
  - g = gravity
  
**Critical Slope (S_c):**
- Channel slope at which uniform flow occurs at critical depth
- Flow is neither subcritical nor supercritical
- Formula: S_c = (nآ²g)/R_h^(1/3)
  - n = Manning's roughness coefficient
  - R_h = hydraulic radius

**Flow Classifications:**
| Condition | Slope Type | Flow Type | Fr |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| S > S_c | Steep | Supercritical | Fr > 1 |
| S = S_c | Critical | Critical | Fr = 1 |
| S < S_c | Mild | Subcritical | Fr < 1 |

### Q13: What is a hydraulic jump? Where does it occur?
**A:**
**Definition:**
- Sudden, turbulent transition from supercritical to subcritical flow
- Rapid rise in water surface
- Significant energy dissipation

**Where it Occurs:**
1. Downstream of spillway gates (rapid flow at bottom)
2. Below sluice gates in canals
3. Where high-speed flow meets obstruction

**Energy Loss in Jump:**
- Large amount of kinetic energy â†’ heat, turbulence, noise
